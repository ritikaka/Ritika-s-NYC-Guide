1. Central Park

Looking for a scenic escape from New York traffic and crowds? With 843 acres of green space, Central Park is the perfect place for everything from casual walks to picnics on the famous Sheep Meadow. The park also houses an ice skating rink (seasonal) zoo, restaurants, rowing (seasonal), volleyball courts, horse drawn carriage rides, and endless routes to traverse. Click here for more information.



2. The MET

The 146-year-old museum carries collections from world-renowned artists like Pablo Picasso and Claude Monet while also showcasing collections from around the world, such as Egypt, China, and Greece. The steps in front of the magnificent structure also serve as a great photo spot and resting space. In addition, admission to NYC museums are based on "suggested donations" so pay what you like! Click here for more information.



3. Brooklyn Botanic Gardens

With spring's arrival, the Brooklyn Botanic Gardens is blooming with diverse and colorful flora. The garden is most notable for its cherry blossoms and annual cherry blossom festival, Sakura Matsuri, happening April 29-30. If you're unable to attend the festival, take advantage of the gardens' free admission on Tuesdays and Saturdays from 10 am to 12 pm. Click here for more information.



4. Flushing

70% of Flushing's population is Asian, making the neighborhood a herald of Asian culture and cuisine. The surge of Chinese immigrants in the 1970s allowed the neighborhood to flourish into one of the most religiously diverse communities. Take the 7 train to Main Street and indulge in endless Chinese and Korean food, shopping, and some of the best bubble tea around. The neighborhood is also home to the oldest Hindu temple in America, Citi field, and the Arthur Ashe Stadium. Click here for more information.



5. Roosevelt Island Tram

On the East River tucked between Manhattan and Queens is the Roosevelt Island Tram. If you're looking to avoid paying extravagant prices for bus, boat, or helicopter tours around the city then the tram is the perfect alternative. With just a swipe of a MetroCard, the Roosevelt Island Tram offers you breathtaking views across the East River of Manhattan and the Queensborough Bridge. Click here for more information.



6. 9/11 Memorial

Constructed along the former location of the Twin Towers, the National September 11 Memorial and Museum (also known as the 9/11 Memorial) pays tribute to the victims of the September 11 attacks. The site includes the names of the lives lost inscribed on bronze plates around the walls of the memorial waterfalls where the Twin Towers once stood, a memorial park, and an underground museum. Click here for more information.



7. Brooklyn Bridge

An iconic symbol of New York, the Brooklyn Bridge is one of the oldest bridges in the US and a designated National Historic Landmark. Connecting the boroughs of Manhattan and Brooklyn, the bridge is a popular destination among tourists and New Yorkers alike for its landscape views and pedestrian-friendly walkways. On the Brooklyn side of the bridge is also the 85-acre Brooklyn Bridge Park. Click here for more information.



8. Bronx Zoo

As the largest metropolitan zoo in the US and among the world, the Bronx Zoo is an attraction great for all ages. Housing over 4,000 animals from 650 different species, some of the zoo's most popular exhibits include the Congo Gorilla Forest, World of Reptiles, Tiger Mountain, and African Plains. The zoo offers General Admission and "Total Experience" tickets, which gives you access to premium exhibits if you're looking for more wildlife adventure. Click here for more information.



9. Astoria

Astoria can be described in one word: food. With a rich immigrant history, the neighborhood is home to diverse ethnic cuisine and trendy experimental food spots as both new and old restaurants line 30th Avenue, Broadway, and Astoria Boulevard. Other places of interest in Astoria are the Museum of the Moving Image, Kaufman Astoria Studios, Astoria Park, and Socrates Sculpture Park. Click here for more information.



10. Statue of Liberty

As one of the most recognized symbols around the world, the Statue of Liberty is a must-see tourist attraction. With the reopening of the statue to the public in 2013, visitors can once again ascend to the crown for stunning views of the New York Harbor, with a reservation. A Circle Line ticket to Liberty Island also includes admission to Ellis Island. Construction of a new Statue of Liberty museum is currently underway, and is scheduled to open in 2019. Click here for more information.
